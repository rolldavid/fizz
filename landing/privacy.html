Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 9, 2026 · applies to the Fizz browser extension

The short version: we collect nothing. Fizz has no servers, no analytics, no telemetry, and no accounts. Everything it stores, it stores on your device, encrypted.

What the extension stores locally

  • Your encrypted vault — the 12-word recovery phrase encrypted with AES-256-GCM under a key derived from your passkey or passphrase.
  • Wallet metadata — contacts, known senders, pending bridge claims, account labels — encrypted at rest under a key derived from your seed.
  • Non-sensitive settings (selected network, token display list).
  • The Aztec PXE database (notes, sync state) in IndexedDB.

None of this leaves your device.

What the extension transmits

Fizz's main network counterpart is the Aztec node you select — a public node we preconfigure, or any node you choose. Like any light wallet, that node necessarily sees your IP address, the addresses you query, and the transactions you submit. It also fetches one-time proving parameters from Aztec's CRS CDN (crs.aztec-cdn.foundation) on your first transaction. We operate no node and receive no traffic ourselves.

Running your own node: to keep your seed safe, the extension only permits network traffic to localhost and *.aztec-labs.com. A self-hosted node therefore has to be reachable on localhost (for example via an SSH tunnel), or you build the extension with your node's address added — a remote node on any other host is blocked by the extension's content-security policy rather than silently trusted.

If you open the testnet faucet, that is a normal website visit to its operator — we deliberately do not include your address in the link.

If you bridge from L1 (on fizzwallet.com/bridge), the L1 transaction is public, like any Ethereum transaction, and links your L1 address to the funded Aztec address. That page also contacts a public Sepolia RPC and, if you pick the WalletConnect option, WalletConnect's relay. Fizz warns about the linkage in the flow; for privacy, fund the L1 side from an exchange or faucet.

Multiple accounts: separate accounts are unlinkable on the Aztec layer, but funding two of them from the same L1 wallet links them on L1, and a shared node can correlate them by IP. Use a fresh L1 funding source per account, and your own node, if you need them kept apart.

What we (the developers) receive

Nothing. We have no way to identify you, contact you, or see your activity.

Changes

Material changes to this policy ship with an extension update and a changelog entry.
